Fashion Designer and Pattern Maker — 22 years creating garments and patterns at the Fashion House of Carolina Herrera, behind garments worn by Mrs. Carolina Herrera, Anna Wintour, U.S. First Ladies, and the world's most discerning clientele during the golden era of one of New York's most iconic fashion houses. Over two decades working New York Fashion Week from the inside — first in the design department constructing the runway collections, then in production where those same garments are refined, graded, and released into the world. Design first, then production. Two departments most people never cross between.

That precision never leaves me — it lives in my hands, and it speaks through my camera.

Published in ENZOMNIA Magazine, Spring 2026, Issue 128 — Barcelona, and Selin Magazine, 2026; and ROOSA Magazine, 2026. Currently a Quarter-Finalist in The People's Artist, a national art competition presented by Johnny Depp.

To me, life is a fashion show — every person, every street corner, every meal carries elegance, color, glamour, and vida. My camera is a compression of my vision and light, built to capture the soul standing in front of me. Whether I'm shooting a child, a grandmother, a cook behind smoke, an athlete who refuses to give up, or anyone who thinks today is just another day — I already see what they don't.

My lens sees only one thing — soul.

Currently developing Las Abuelitas — Their Hands Feed Us and Protect Us, a long-term editorial project photographing grandmothers across cultures — hands that fed families, healed wounds, built homes, and carried entire worlds without ever asking for recognition.

Based in New York City. Available for editorial, fashion, sports, portrait, and event photography.
